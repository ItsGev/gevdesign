Case Study · UX/UI · Travel

Unified Viking
Experience

Seamless travel, all in one place

Project Overview

This project aimed to transform Viking's digital ecosystem by rebuilding the My Viking Journey (MVJ) website and the Viking Voyager App into a unified, seamless digital experience. The goal was to enhance every stage of the guest journey — before, during, and after a cruise — by eliminating functional gaps, addressing usability issues, and meeting the growing expectations for mobile-enabled experiences.

Contribution

My Contribution

I was involved from the early stages, collaborating with Viking and an external design agency hired for the initial work. During onboarding I participated in key meetings, provided insights into existing user experiences, and facilitated a smooth handoff of our design system and style guide. Once the agency completed their deliverables, I took ownership of the project and drove it through to completion.

Problem

Problem

  • Disjointed User Experience — The existing app and website lacked cohesion, creating challenges during key phases like booking, in-transit, and post-trip.
  • Low Adoption and Awareness — Guests depended on call centers and onboard staff due to subpar digital experiences.
  • Functional Gaps — Limited features and inconsistent performance led to gaps in user engagement, especially during offline periods.
Goal

Goal

To develop a mobile-first digital companion platform that:

  • Empowers Guests — With tools for itinerary customization, travel updates, and self-service capabilities.
  • Unifies the Ecosystem — A seamless experience across all phases of the guest journey, including offline functionality.
  • Enhances User Adoption — Reduces dependency on external support and onboard teams.
  • Leverages Data — Employs analytics to personalize experiences, improve functionality, and drive innovation.
Solution

Solution

  • Consolidated Platform — The new MVJ app merges the website and app into a single digital companion.
  • Expanded Capabilities — Self-service features, offline access to cached content, and integration with travel services.
  • Improved Adoption — Push notifications, personalized content, and seamless logins to ensure greater engagement.
  • Analytics-Driven Development — Insights into user behavior inform ongoing improvements.
Process

Process

1

Start with what we understand

We pinpointed core pain points and business objectives, established KPIs, and focused on search categories and touchpoints to guide our efforts forward.

2

Mapping the Journey

By mapping the site's hierarchy we identified opportunities to streamline navigation, reduce friction, and guide users toward key actions effortlessly.

3

Existing Designs

The App and MVJ operated as two distinct products with overlapping functionality. This created issues with Visual Identity — lacking cohesive branding — and Accessibility — requiring users to juggle two separate products.

4

High-Fidelity Mocks (Part 1)

The design agency produced high-fidelity designs that were aesthetically strong but fell short of our UX goals. Despite the limitations, they provided an excellent foundational starting point.

5

High-Fidelity Mocks (Part 2)

I designed the outstanding key components — Gamification, Login/Create Account, a dynamic Message Center, next cruise booking, and more. These were essential to delivering the full potential of the unified platform.
