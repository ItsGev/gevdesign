Case Study · UX/UI · Hospitality

Hilton
Microsite

An interactive Content Hub to boost engagement and outshine competitors

Introduction

Hilton Hotels & Resorts remains the stylish, forward-thinking global leader of hospitality — aiding easier travel with smart design, innovative restaurant concepts, authentic hospitality, and commitment to the global economy.

Goal

Goal

Entice users through fun and interactive components throughout the microsite — offering a variety of experiences such as animations, carousels, and widgets.

Problem

Problem

Hilton's mobile user demographic (ages 18–35) is highly active on publisher websites such as Fodor's Travel, Reader's Digest, and Lonely Planet. These sites follow a predictable formula of imagery, copy, hyperlinks, and banners — creating a significant engagement problem. Taboola reported the average time spent on an individual publisher page dropped from 2.09 minutes in Q1 2017 to 1.90 minutes in Q4 of the same year.

Solution

Solution

To address declining engagement, we introduced an interactive Content Hub designed to keep users on article pages longer. The hub would be educational, engaging, and uniquely positioned to stand out from the competition.

Process

Process

1

Kick Off with Product Manager

Defined project scope, asked questions, established user personas and their goals, and reviewed customer requests and feedback.

2

Research

Conducted competitive analysis with screenshots for similar features, noting advantages and disadvantages. Interviewed internal and external users, then compared and contrasted findings.

Inspiration

3

Sketches & Wireframes

Structured content around the full customer journey, designed the series of steps the end user would take, and presented initial ideas to product managers and potential users.

4

Testing

Results

Conclusion

The Content Hub provides a new and innovative way of advertising. Users are effectively enticed to engage with the content, leading to more bookings and increased brand awareness.
