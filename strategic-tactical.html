Strategic Tactical
Journey

Embracing newcomers and welcoming returning explorers

Project Overview

This project aimed to educate users, new and old, about the value of a Viking cruise, while also showcasing current offerings. For many users, understanding the full cruise experience can be a challenge — especially those new to the brand or who have never cruised before, resulting in lower conversion rates. As mobile usage grows, the solution had to work seamlessly across all breakpoints.

My Contribution

As the main designer on this project, I had full responsibility for ensuring the final product met all requirements. My focus was creating a user-centered design that provided an intuitive and seamless experience — conducting user research, creating wireframes, producing final UI designs, and building prototypes.

Problem

The existing design appeared outdated and lacked user appeal, potentially eroding trust. Moreover, the absence of informative content hampered users' understanding of the Viking cruise experience.

To address these issues, I recommended incorporating genuine customer testimonials for authenticity and showcasing the comprehensive value of Viking's offerings. A visual update would make the page more inviting and user-friendly.

This holistic strategy aimed to establish trust, resonate with users, and drive conversions — blending content enhancement with visual refinement.

Goal

  • Create added value of the offer and the Viking experience
  • Optimize the urgency of the offer details
  • Increase booking conversions (emphasis on new-to-brand)
  • Reduce bounce rate

Solution

  • Testimonials from satisfied customers — humanize the experience and showcase the unique value Viking offers
  • Inclusive Value — highlight that Viking includes no extra fees or hidden charges
  • User-Friendly Design — visually appealing, easy to navigate, aligned with Viking's brand identity
  • Educational Content — provide insights into the cruise experience and itineraries to help users make informed decisions

Process

1

Kick Off with a Product Manager

  • Both myself and the product manager established the goal of the kick-off: align on project requirements, objectives, and expectations
  • The product manager provided an overview of the project including purpose, target audience, market positioning, and strategic goals
  • Shared relevant user research findings, personas, and customer insights
  • Discussed scope, deliverables, timeline, milestones, technical constraints, team size, and KPIs
2

Research

  • Analyzed the target user groups, demographics, preferences, needs, and behavior patterns
  • Conducted competitive and comparative research on existing special offer experiences
  • Reviewed previous user interviews and analyzed the insights gathered

4

Low-Fidelity Mocks

This is where everything starts to click. Low-fidelity mocks help the team and stakeholders visualize the product and align on direction. During this phase, we removed some sections, added new ones, and simplified others — for example, the Discover Our Ships section was condensed into a highlight rather than a full section.

5

High-Fidelity Mocks

With UX finalized, I applied our design system and branding to produce pixel-perfect high-fidelity mockups across desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Testing

I set up a test on Usertesting.com to gather feedback before launch. Based on results, I iterated on the design — for example, replacing a carousel arrow on the Itinerary Cards with a more visible "Show More" CTA button that users had been missing.

Conclusion

With this new version, users get a full experience — educated on the Viking brand, the current deal is clear, and all available itineraries are surfaced at a glance.
